# 01 — PRD: Product Requirements Document

---

## App Name
**Cortex**

## Tagline
Your entire life, one local app. Think, capture, grow.

---

## The Problem
College students are scattered across 5+ tools — Notes app for ideas, Google Calendar for schedule, Notion for projects, a random journal app, and sticky notes for tasks. Nothing talks to each other. Nothing builds on itself. After a full semester, you still have no clear picture of how you've grown, what you've learned, or what you actually want.

---

## Target User
A college student (17–25) juggling coursework, personal projects, side interests, and life admin. They're curious and self-aware but overwhelmed. They want to feel in control without spending 2 hours setting up a system. They care about privacy — they don't want their thoughts and goals syncing to some startup's server.

---

## Core Value Proposition
Everything in one place, fully local, with an AI that actually reads your notes and builds a picture of who you are — automatically. No manual tagging. No organizing. Just capture and let Cortex do the rest.

---

## Core Features — Must Have

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Overview of your day: today's tasks, schedule blocks, recent notes, protocol snapshot |
| **Quick Capture / Inbox** | One-click note dump. No friction. Text lands in inbox instantly |
| **Journal** | Daily writing with optional AI prompts. One entry per day, auto-dated |
| **Wiki / Knowledge Base** | Linked pages. AI processes inbox notes into wiki entries automatically |
| **The Protocol** | Personal profile file — AI auto-updates skills, goals, decisions, patterns, timeline |
| **Todo / Tasks** | Tasks with priority, tags, due dates, and done state |
| **Timetable** | Weekly schedule grid — classes, routines, blocks |
| **Daily Note** | Today view combining tasks, schedule, journal, habits in one screen |
| **AI Brain** | Processes inbox → wiki + updates Protocol. Also answers questions about your notes |
| **Settings** | Set your name, Anthropic API key, app theme, Protocol preferences |

---

## Nice to Have — V2

- 🎵 Music player (local files or lofi stream embed)
- 📂 Projects section — goals, milestones, deadlines
- 📈 Habit tracker with streaks and calendar heatmap
- 🔍 Full-text search across all notes, wiki, journal
- 📎 File / image attachments on notes
- 📊 Growth stats — words written, tasks completed, wiki pages created
- 🌐 Obsidian-style graph view of linked wiki pages
- 📱 Responsive / mobile view

---

## Out of Scope (V1)

- No cloud sync or remote storage
- No user accounts or authentication
- No collaboration or sharing
- No payment or subscriptions
- No mobile-first design
- No third-party calendar integrations (Google Calendar, etc.)
- No email or notification system

---

## User Stories

- As a student, I want to dump a thought in under 3 seconds so I never lose an idea mid-lecture.
- As a student, I want the AI to process my inbox notes into my wiki so I don't have to organize manually.
- As a student, I want to see today's tasks, schedule, and journal all in one view so I don't have to jump between apps.
- As a student, I want a living profile that tracks my skills and goals automatically so I can see my growth over months.
- As a student, I want everything stored locally so my private thoughts never leave my machine.
- As a student, I want to write in my journal daily and have AI suggest reflection prompts when I'm stuck.
- As a student, I want my weekly timetable to show what's on today at a glance.

---

## Success Metrics

- User opens the app daily (daily active use)
- Protocol has 10+ auto-generated entries within the first month
- Inbox is processed at least once per week
- At least 20 wiki pages created within 2 months
- Journal has entries on 15+ days in first month
